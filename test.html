James Patterson

The one time New York Times' best-seller



Here's a time line of James Patterson's life:


  • 1947Patterson was born in Newburgh, New York

  • 1971 - He attended Manhattan College as an undergraduate before starting coursework for a master and took a job as a copywriter at ad agency J. Walter Thompson

  • 1976 - He published his first book, The Thomas Berryman Number which won an Edgar Award, a top prize for mystery writers.

  • 1993 - Patterson used his work experience to orchestrate a television ad campaign for the novel Along Came a Spider, which helped it become a best seller

  • 1996 - Patterson left his life as an executive in order to focus on writing.

  • 2007 - Another popular Patterson series is Women's Murder Club, which was adapted into a short-lived TV show was prroduced.

  • 2018 - He later teamed with former U.S. President Bill Clinton to pen the thriller The President Is Missing
